Immediate Steps (First 24 Hours)

What to do right after the accident

1

Check for Injuries & Call 911

First priority: safety. Check yourself, passengers, and anyone in the other vehicle for injuries. If anyone is hurt, call 911 immediately. Even if injuries seem minor, always report them to emergency services. Some injuries (like whiplash or internal injuries) don't show symptoms right away.

‚ö†Ô∏è Never refuse medical evaluation at the scene

Declining medical attention can hurt your insurance claim later. If paramedics are present, let them evaluate you even if you feel fine.

2

Move to Safety (If Possible)

If the accident is minor and vehicles are drivable, move them out of traffic to prevent additional collisions. Turn on hazard lights. If vehicles can't be moved or the accident is serious, stay where you are and wait for police. Never stand in traffic.

  • Turn on hazard lights
  • Use reflective triangles or flares if available
  • Stay in your vehicle if traffic is heavy
3

Document Everything with Photos

Take photos of everything while details are fresh. Use your phone to capture multiple angles. These photos are crucial for insurance claims and potential legal cases. Get photos before other vehicles move or weather conditions change.

üì∏ What to photograph:

  • ‚Ä¢ All vehicle damage from multiple angles
  • ‚Ä¢ Accident scene and road conditions
  • ‚Ä¢ Traffic signs and signals
  • ‚Ä¢ Skid marks or debris
  • ‚Ä¢ Other vehicles involved (front, back, sides)
  • ‚Ä¢ License plates of all vehicles
  • ‚Ä¢ Overall scene showing intersections or landmarks
4

Call Oakland Police (Non-Emergency)

In Oakland, if there are injuries or significant property damage, you need a police report. Call the Oakland Police Department non-emergency line: (510) 777-3333. A police report creates an official record that's important for insurance and legal purposes.

‚úì Police report benefits:

Provides official documentation, helps establish fault, required by most insurance companies, protects you if the other driver's story changes later.

5

Exchange Information (Don't Admit Fault)

Get the other driver's name, phone number, address, driver's license number, license plate, vehicle make/model, insurance company, and policy number. Also get contact information from any witnesses. Never admit fault or apologize for the accident ‚Äì even a simple "I'm sorry" can be used against you.

‚ö†Ô∏è What NOT to say:

"I didn't see you" ‚Ä¢ "I'm sorry" ‚Ä¢ "It was my fault" ‚Ä¢ "I was distracted" ‚Ä¢ Any admission of responsibility. Keep conversation neutral and factual.

6

Call Your Insurance Company

Report the accident to your insurance company as soon as possible, even if you weren't at fault. Have your policy number ready. Provide facts only ‚Äì don't speculate about fault or injuries. Document the name and claim number of the representative you speak with.

  • Have your policy number ready
  • Stick to the facts
  • Get claim number and representative name

The Critical 72-Hour Window

Why seeing a doctor immediately matters for your health and your claim

‚ö†Ô∏è URGENT: See a Doctor Within 72 Hours

Many serious injuries from car accidents don't show symptoms immediately. Whiplash, soft tissue injuries, and internal injuries can develop over hours or days. Insurance companies use the "72-hour rule" ‚Äì if you wait longer than 72 hours to seek medical care, they may argue your injuries weren't caused by the accident.

Why Delays Hurt Your Claim

  • Claim Denial Risk

    Insurers may deny claims if treatment starts too late

  • Reduced Compensation

    Delayed treatment weakens your injury documentation

  • Missed Diagnosis

    Serious injuries can worsen without early treatment

Common Delayed Injuries

  • Whiplash

    Neck and upper back pain may develop 24-48 hours later

  • Soft Tissue Injuries

    Muscle and ligament damage develops gradually

  • Headaches & Migraines

    Can start days after impact from tension or injury

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions after a car accident

What if the other driver doesn't have insurance?

This is why you need uninsured motorist coverage on your own policy. File a claim with your insurance company under this coverage. Your insurance will handle the claim as if they were insured. Always report the accident to police ‚Äì this creates an official record that helps your claim.

Should I accept the other driver's insurance settlement offer immediately?

No. Initial settlement offers are often lower than what you deserve. Don't accept until you:

  • ‚Ä¢ Have completed medical treatment
  • ‚Ä¢ Have documented all injuries and expenses
  • ‚Ä¢ Understand the full extent of your injuries
  • ‚Ä¢ Have consulted with a personal injury attorney (many offer free consultations)

Once you accept a settlement, you typically can't sue for additional damages later.

How long do I have to file a claim in Oakland?

In California, the statute of limitations for personal injury claims is 2 years from the date of the accident. However, don't wait this long. File your claim as soon as possible:

  • ‚Ä¢ Report to insurance within 30 days
  • ‚Ä¢ Begin medical treatment within 72 hours
  • ‚Ä¢ Consult an attorney within 6 months if considering a lawsuit

Delays make it harder to prove your case.

Can I use my health insurance instead of auto insurance?

You can use your health insurance, but your auto insurance should cover it. Auto insurance medical payments coverage typically covers 100% of medical expenses with no deductible, while health insurance may require deductibles and copays. Always use auto insurance first for car accident injuries. Your health insurance may also seek reimbursement later (subrogation).

What if I feel fine after the accident but develop pain later?

This is extremely common. Even if you feel fine at the scene, see a doctor within 72 hours anyway. Many injuries develop gradually. If you wait weeks or months to seek treatment, the insurance company may argue the injury wasn't caused by the accident. Getting evaluated early protects your claim, even if you're asymptomatic.

Do I need a personal injury attorney?

For minor accidents with small injuries and clear liability, you may not need one. However, if you have significant injuries, the other driver disputes fault, or the insurance company undervalues your claim, an attorney is invaluable. Most personal injury attorneys work on contingency (no upfront cost, they take a percentage of your settlement). Many offer free consultations to evaluate your case.
